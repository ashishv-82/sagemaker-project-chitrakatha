आदर्श चित्र कथा (Adarsh Chitra Katha) - Comprehensive Bilingual Encyclopedia

Publication Overview (प्रकाशन परिचय):
Hindi: आदर्श चित्र कथा भारतीय कॉमिक्स के इतिहास में एक महत्वपूर्ण लेकिन अल्पज्ञात प्रकाशन है। नई दिल्ली के आरगस सेंट्रल इंटरप्राइजेज द्वारा प्रकाशित यह श्रृंखला जुलाई 1979 में शुरू हुई और नवम्बर 1984 तक चली। इसके कुल 46 अंक प्रकाशित हुए जो हिंदी और अंग्रेजी दोनों भाषाओं में एक साथ उपलब्ध थे।

English: Adarsh Chitra Katha is an important but lesser-known publication in the history of Indian comics. Published by Argus Central Enterprises of New Delhi, this series ran from July 1979 to November 1984, producing a total of 46 issues simultaneously in both Hindi and English.

Historical Context (ऐतिहासिक संदर्भ):
Hindi: अमर चित्र कथा से पहले भारतीय कॉमिक्स प्रकाशकों की रुचि केवल विदेशी कॉमिक्सों या उनसे प्रभावित चरित्रों को प्रकाशित करने की रही थी। अमर चित्र कथा के प्रकाशक इंडिया बुक हाउस ने भी पहले के दस अंकों में यही किया था जब उन्होंने विश्व प्रसिद्ध परीकथाओं को प्रकाशित किया था। परन्तु 11वे अंक से उन्होंने भारतीय संस्कृति, इतिहास, पुराणों और लोक-समाज में प्रचलित कथाओं को चित्रकथाओं के रूप में प्रकाशित करना शुरू किया। भारतीय रंगों में रंगी अमर चित्र कथाओं को भारत भर के पाठकों का अभूतपूर्व प्यार मिला और इसी अविश्वसनीय सफलता ने अन्य प्रकाशन गृहों को भी भारतीय गाथाओं को चित्रकथाओं के रंगीन पन्नों में लाने के लिए प्रेरित किया।

English: Before Amar Chitra Katha established the Indian mythology comic genre, publishers in India focused almost exclusively on reprinting foreign comics or creating characters inspired by them. Even India Book House, the publisher of Amar Chitra Katha, spent its first ten issues on world fairy tales before pivoting to Indian mythology from issue eleven onwards. The extraordinary public reception of those Indian-themed stories inspired other publishers — including Argus Central Enterprises — to enter the market with their own mythology-based comic series.

Editorial Quality and Comparison (संपादकीय गुणवत्ता):
Hindi: आदर्श चित्रकथा अमर चित्र कथाओं से संपादन और कथा कहने के दृष्टिकोण से बेहतर मानी जाती थी। इसके पहले अंक 'नौ द्वारों की नगरी' में प्रताप मुलिक की शानदार चित्रकला देखने को मिली, जो भागवत पुराण की एक कथा पर आधारित थी। दुर्भाग्यवश अमर चित्र कथा के व्यापक प्रचार-प्रसार और इंडिया बुक हाउस के बड़े प्रकाशन नेटवर्क के सामने यह प्रकाशन टिक नहीं पाया और जल्द ही इतिहास का हिस्सा बन गया।

English: By many accounts, Adarsh Chitra Katha was editorially superior to Amar Chitra Katha in its storytelling approach and narrative depth. Its debut issue, "The City of Nine Gates" (Nau Dwaron Ki Nagari), drawn by the celebrated illustrator Pratap Mulik and based on a tale from the Bhagavata Purana, was widely praised. Unfortunately, it could not compete with Amar Chitra Katha's extensive distribution network and the marketing power of India Book House, and it ceased publication relatively early in its life.

Format and Pricing (प्रारूप और मूल्य):
Hindi: आदर्श चित्र कथा के सारे अंक बड़े आकार में 32 रंगीन पृष्ठों में प्रकाशित हुए थे। शुरूआती 20 चित्रकथाओं (अंक 1–20) का मूल्य 2.50 रुपये था। अंक 21 से 40 तक का मूल्य 3.00 रुपये था। उसके बाद के पाँच अंक 3.50 रुपये में आये और अंतिम अंक स्वामी शिवानन्द 4.00 रुपये मूल्य के साथ नवम्बर 1984 में प्रकाशित हुआ। यह श्रृंखला हिंदी और अंग्रेजी दोनों ही भाषाओँ में साथ-साथ प्रकाशित होती रही।

English: All issues of Adarsh Chitra Katha were published in a large format with 32 full-colour pages. Issues 1 to 20 were priced at Rs 2.50 each. Issues 21 to 40 were priced at Rs 3.00. The next five issues (41–45) cost Rs 3.50, and the final issue, Swami Shivananda (No. 46), was priced at Rs 4.00. Every issue was released simultaneously in both Hindi and English editions.

Publication Schedule (प्रकाशन कार्यक्रम):
Hindi: आदर्श चित्र कथा अपने अंतिम कुछ अंकों को छोड़कर प्रतिमाह प्रकाशित होती रही थी। 1979 से 1982 तक कुछ अपवादों को छोड़कर यह नियमित रूप से प्रकाशित होती रही। लेकिन 1983 में इसके केवल चार अंक आये और अंतिम वर्ष 1984 में नवम्बर के अंतिम अंक तक केवल तीन ही अंक प्रकाशित हो पाए। अंतिम अंकों के समय शायद प्रकाशन को बंद करने का निश्चय पहले ही किया जा चुका था, अन्यथा प्रत्येक चित्रकथा में आने वाली चित्रकथा का उल्लेख अवश्य होता था, परन्तु आखिरी के वर्षों में यह परंपरा बंद कर दी गई।

English: Adarsh Chitra Katha maintained a monthly publication schedule through most of its run. From 1979 to 1982 it published regularly with only minor gaps. However, publication frequency declined sharply in 1983, with only four issues released that year. In its final year 1984, only three issues appeared before the series ended with its November issue. The decision to close the series appears to have been made well before the last issue, as the practice of announcing the next issue inside each comic — a standard feature throughout the run — was quietly discontinued in the final years.

Collector's Value (संग्राहक के लिए महत्व):
Hindi: भले ही आदर्श चित्र कथा के केवल 46 अंक (ज्ञात जानकारी के अनुसार) आये थे लेकिन ये सभी अंक किसी भी कॉमिक्स संग्राहक के लिए एक शानदार धरोहर हैं। इसकी दुर्लभता और उच्च संपादकीय गुणवत्ता इसे भारतीय कॉमिक्स संग्रह में विशेष स्थान दिलाती है।

English: Although Adarsh Chitra Katha ran for only 46 known issues, every single issue is considered a prized collectible. Its rarity combined with its acknowledged editorial quality gives it a special place in any serious Indian comics collection.

Complete Issue List (सम्पूर्ण अंक सूची):

Issue 1: THE CITY OF NINE GATES / नौ द्वारों की नगरी — July 1979 (जुलाई 1979)
Source: Bhagavata Purana (भागवत पुराण). Illustrated by Pratap Mulik (प्रताप मुलिक). This was the debut issue of the series, telling the Puranic allegory of the body as a city with nine gates. The art by Mulik was widely praised for its detail and expressiveness.

Issue 2: RISHYASRINGA / ऋष्यश्रृंग — August 1979 (अगस्त 1979)
The story of Rishyasringa, the forest-born sage who had never encountered a woman. His meeting with Princess Shanta and the subsequent events form this tale drawn from ancient Indian texts.

Issue 3: BALARAMA TO THE RESCUE / बलराम की विजय — September 1979 (सितम्बर 1979)
A tale of Balarama, elder brother of Lord Krishna, showcasing his strength and devotion.

Issue 4: SADHU AND LILAVATI / साधू और लीलावती — October 1979 (अक्टूबर 1979)
A morality tale featuring a holy man and a woman named Lilavati, exploring themes of devotion and temptation.

Issue 5: BRAHMA'S INSTRUCTIONS TO GODS, MEN AND DEMONS / देव, मानव और दानव को ब्रम्हा का आदेश — November 1979 (नवम्बर 1979)
Based on the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad story of Prajapati Brahma giving the syllable 'Da' to gods, humans, and demons, each interpreting it differently.

Issue 6: BATTLE OF WITS / अकबर और बीरबल — December 1979 (दिसम्बर 1979)
Tales of the legendary wit and wisdom of Birbal, the favourite minister of the Mughal Emperor Akbar. One of the few non-mythological entries in the series.

Issue 7: AHALYA / अहल्या — January 1980 (जनवरी 1980)
Source: Ramayana (रामायण). The story of Ahalya, wife of sage Gautama, who was cursed to become a stone and was later liberated by the touch of Lord Rama's feet.

Issue 8: BHADRA KUNDALAKESHA / भद्रा कुण्डलकेशा — February 1980 (फरवरी 1980)
The story of Bhadra Kundalakesha, one of the eleven chief female disciples of the Buddha. A tale of spiritual transformation and philosophical debate.

Issue 9: JASSA SINGH AHLUWALIA / जस्सा सिंह अहलूवालिया — March 1980 (मार्च 1980)
A historical biography of Jassa Singh Ahluwalia (1718–1783), the supreme commander of the Dal Khalsa and founder of the Sikh kingdom of Kapurthala. One of the few historical (non-mythological) issues.

Issue 10: KING PRASENJITA AND HIS SON / राजा प्रसन्नजीत और उनका पुत्र — April 1980 (अप्रैल 1980)
A Buddhist Jataka-influenced tale about the king of Kosala and his son, exploring themes of justice, wisdom, and royal duty.

Issue 11: THE SONS OF BHARADWAJA AND RAIBHYA / भारद्वाज और रैभ्या के पुत्र — May 1980 (मई 1980)
A tale from the Mahabharata tradition about rivalry and conflict between the sons of two great sages, Bharadwaja and Raibhya.

Issue 12: VED VYASA / वेदव्यास — June 1980 (जून 1980)
A biographical comic on Krishna Dvaipayana Vyasa, the sage credited with composing the Mahabharata and compiling the four Vedas into their present form.

Issue 13: TALES OF HANUMAN / श्री हनुमान की कथायें — July 1980 (जुलाई 1980)
A collection of stories celebrating the devotion, strength, and wisdom of Lord Hanuman, the divine monkey warrior of the Ramayana.

Issue 14: THE FALSE HERMIT AND OTHER STORIES / ढोंगी साधू तथा अन्य कहानियाँ — August 1980 (अगस्त 1980)
An anthology issue featuring moral tales including the story of a fake holy man whose deceptions are eventually exposed.

Issue 15: SANTOSHI MATA / संतोषी माता — September 1980 (सितम्बर 1980)
The popular folk goddess Santoshi Mata, whose worship gained widespread popularity in India in the 1970s following the hit Bollywood film. The comic retells her origin and the significance of her Friday fast rituals.

Issue 16: SWAMI RAMTIRTHA / स्वामी रामतीर्थ — October 1980 (अक्टूबर 1980)
A biographical issue on Swami Rama Tirtha (1873–1906), the Vedanta philosopher and spiritual teacher who lectured extensively in Japan and the United States.

Issue 17: KATTHAHARI JATAK / कथाहारी जातक — November 1980 (नवम्बर 1980)
A story from the Jataka tales, the ancient collection of stories about the previous lives of the Gautama Buddha.

Issue 18: JAISAL AND TORAL / जैसल और तोरल — December 1980 (दिसम्बर 1980)
A romantic folk legend from Rajasthan and Sindh about the dacoit Jaisal and the faithful Toral, a tale celebrated in folk songs across the region.

Issue 19: A FOOL'S LUCK / मूर्ख का भाग्य — January 1981 (जनवरी 1981)
A humorous folk tale about a simpleton whose blundering leads to unexpected fortune, exploring the theme of luck versus wisdom.

Issue 20: SAI BABA OF SHIRDI / शिरडी के साई बाबा — February 1981 (फरवरी 1981)
A devotional biography of Shirdi Sai Baba (died 1918), the revered saint of Shirdi whose teachings blended Hindu and Sufi Muslim traditions.

Issue 21: JADA BHARATA / जड़ भारत — March 1981 (मार्च 1981)
Source: Bhagavata Purana. The story of Bharata, a great king who gave up his throne to pursue liberation but was reborn as a deer due to his attachment. In his third life as the avadhuta Jada Bharata, he attains final liberation.

Issue 22: VISHNU SAHASRANAMA OF 999 NAMES / ९९९ नामों का विष्णु सहस्त्र नाम — April 1981 (अप्रैल 1981)
A unique issue presenting the thousand names of Lord Vishnu drawn from the Mahabharata, with illustrations depicting the meanings of each divine name.

Issue 23: THE ADVENT OF SRI KRISHNA / श्री कृष्णावतार — May 1981 (मई 1981)
The story of the birth and early childhood of Lord Krishna — his miraculous birth in prison, the exchange of infants to save him from Kamsa, and his upbringing in Gokul.

Issue 24: KING JANAKA / राजा जनक — June 1981 (जून 1981)
A portrayal of the philosopher-king Janaka of Mithila, father of Sita and one of the most celebrated Rajrishis (royal sages) in Hindu tradition, known for his detachment despite his royal duties.

Issue 25: AANDAL / आँडल — July 1981 (जुलाई 1981)
The life of Andal (Kodhai), the only female Alvar saint among the twelve Vaishnava poet-saints of South India. Known for her passionate devotional poetry, the Tiruppavai, addressed to Lord Vishnu.

Issue 26: KANYAKUMARI / कन्याकुमारी — August 1981 (अगस्त 1981)
The legend of Kanyakumari, the virgin goddess at the southernmost tip of India who waited eternally for her divine bridegroom and whose unfulfilled marriage feast became the coloured sands of the cape.

Issue 27: THE TWELVE JYOTIRLINGAS / बारह ज्योतिर्लिंग — September 1981 (सितम्बर 1981)
A guide to the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva — the most sacred pilgrimage sites in Shaivite Hinduism — explaining the legend behind each temple.

Issue 28: THE DOVE AND THE HUNTER / कबूतर और शिकारी — October 1981 (अक्टूबर 1981)
A moral tale from ancient Indian literature about compassion, sacrifice, and the consequences of violence, featuring a hunter and a devoted pair of doves.

Issue 29: THE TALES OF GANESHA / गणेश की कथायें — November 1981 (नवम्बर 1981)
An anthology of popular stories about the elephant-headed deity Ganesha — his birth, his broken tusk, the race around the world with his brother Kartikeya, and other beloved tales.

Issue 30: POT OF GOLD / स्वर्ण पात्र — December 1981 (दिसम्बर 1981)
A folk tale about the discovery of a pot of gold and the moral dilemmas, greed, and consequences that follow its discovery in a village community.

Issue 31: KARDAMA AND DEVAHUTI / कर्दमा और देवाहुति — January 1982 (जनवरी 1982)
Source: Bhagavata Purana. The story of the sage Kardama and his wife Devahuti, parents of the great sage Kapila, who propounded the Samkhya school of philosophy.

Issue 32: TALES FROM THE SHIVA PURANA / शिव पुराण की कथायें — March 1982 (मार्च 1982)
An anthology of significant episodes from the Shiva Purana, one of the eighteen major Puranas of Hinduism, celebrating the many forms and deeds of Lord Shiva.

Issue 33: DHRUVA / ध्रुव — April 1982 (अप्रैल 1982)
Source: Vishnu Purana and Bhagavata Purana. The story of Prince Dhruva, the five-year-old boy who was denied his father's lap by his stepmother and went alone into the forest to perform austerities until he attained a direct vision of Lord Vishnu and became the Pole Star.

Issue 34: SUDARSHAN THE WARRIOR / महावीर सुदर्शन — May 1982 (मई 1982)
The tale of Sudarshan, a heroic warrior whose devotion, courage, and righteousness are tested through a series of challenges.

Issue 35: ROAR LION ROAR / गरज शेर गरज — June 1982 (जून 1982)
An adventure tale featuring a lion as the central character, likely drawn from the Panchatantra or a regional folk tradition, exploring themes of courage and the natural world.

Issue 36: THE PIGEON AND THE JACKAL AND OTHER STORIES / कबूतर और स्यार — July 1982 (जुलाई 1982)
An anthology featuring animal fables in the tradition of the Panchatantra and Hitopadesha, including the title story of a pigeon and a jackal.

Issue 37: THE SIEGE OF RANTHAMBOR / रंथम्बोर का घेराव — September 1982 (सितम्बर 1982)
A historical comic based on the siege of Ranthambore Fort, the famous Rajput stronghold in present-day Rajasthan, capturing the heroism and sacrifice of its defenders.

Issue 38: KING PARI / सम्राट पारी — October 1982 (अक्टूबर 1982)
The story of Pari (Paari), the legendary Tamil chieftain of the Sangam era celebrated in classical Tamil poetry for his extraordinary generosity. He is said to have given his chariot to a jasmine vine that had no support to climb.

Issue 39: PRITHVIVALLABH / पृथ्वी वल्लभ — November 1982 (नवम्बर 1982)
Based on the famous historical novel by Kanaiyalal Maneklal Munshi (K.M. Munshi), this issue dramatises the story of the Chalukya king Tailapa II and his complex relationships in medieval Deccan India.

Issue 40: THE PAUPER PRINCE / दरिद्र राजकुमार — January 1983 (जनवरी 1983)
A tale of a prince who loses his kingdom and wealth but retains his dignity, wisdom, and noble character through adversity — a theme common to several Puranic and folk narratives.

Issue 41: AMBARISHA / अम्बरीश — March 1983 (मार्च 1983)
Source: Bhagavata Purana. The story of King Ambarisha, the great devotee of Lord Vishnu, whose steadfast devotion protected him even against the wrath of the sage Durvasa.

Issue 42: THE MONKEY PRINCE / वानर राजकुमार — April-May 1983 (अप्रैल-मई 1983)
A tale from Indian folklore about a prince born with monkey-like characteristics, exploring themes of acceptance, courage, and inner nobility over outward appearance.

Issue 43: SRI VENKATESWARA / श्री वेंकटेश्वर — August 1983 (अगस्त 1983)
The legend of Lord Venkateswara (Balaji) of Tirupati — one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in the world — tracing the divine story of how Vishnu came to reside on the Tirumala hills.

Issue 44: THE NAYANAR SAINTS / नयनार के संत — February 1984 (फरवरी 1984)
Stories of the Nayanmars, the 63 Tamil Shaivite poet-saints whose devotional hymns collected in the Tevaram form the cornerstone of the Tamil Bhakti tradition.

Issue 45: THE PROUD POTTER AND OTHER STORIES / घमंडी कुम्हार — May 1984 (मई 1984)
An anthology of moral folk tales including the title story of an arrogant potter whose pride leads to his downfall, drawn from the Indian oral storytelling tradition.

Issue 46: SWAMI SHIVANANDA / स्वामी शिवानन्द — November 1984 (नवम्बर 1984)
The final issue of the series. A devotional biography of Swami Sivananda Saraswati (1887–1963), the revered spiritual teacher and yoga master who founded the Divine Life Society in Rishikesh. His teachings on health, yoga, and Vedanta continue to influence practitioners worldwide.

Languages: Hindi (हिंदी) and English (अंग्रेजी) — published simultaneously
Format: Large format, 32 colour pages per issue
Run: July 1979 – November 1984
Total Issues: 46 (known)
Price Range: Rs 2.50 (issues 1–20) to Rs 4.00 (final issue)
Primary Content: Indian mythology, Puranas, history, biography, and folk tales
